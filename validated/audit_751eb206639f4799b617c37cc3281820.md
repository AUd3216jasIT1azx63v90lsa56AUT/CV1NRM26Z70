### Title
Lenders Can Front-Run Bad Debt Socialization to Avoid Proportional Credit Slashing — (File: `src/Midnight.sol`)

---

### Summary

When a borrower's bad debt is realized via `liquidate`, the market's `lossFactor` is updated, and all lenders are expected to absorb a proportional credit loss at their next `_updatePosition` call. However, because the slash is applied lazily and `withdraw` does not atomically lock lenders out before the `lossFactor` update, a lender who observes a pending `liquidate` transaction in the mempool can front-run it with a `withdraw` call. This exits their credit at the pre-bad-debt `lossFactor`, transferring their share of the loss entirely onto remaining lenders.

---

### Finding Description

**Vulnerability class:** State transition / accounting — front-running (TOCTOU on `lossFactor`)

**Root cause:**

Bad debt socialization in Morpho Midnight is a two-step, lazy process:

1. **Step 1 — `liquidate` updates `lossFactor`** (`src/Midnight.sol` lines 626–641): When `badDebt > 0`, `_marketState.lossFactor` is increased proportionally.

2. **Step 2 — `_updatePosition` applies the slash** (`src/Midnight.sol` lines 805–806): Each lender's credit is reduced by `credit * (MAX - currentLossFactor) / (MAX - lastLossFactor)` the next time they interact.

The critical gap: **between Step 1 and Step 2, a lender can call `withdraw`**, which itself calls `_updatePosition` (line 485) — but at that point `lossFactor` has not yet been updated. So `currentLossFactor == lastLossFactor`, the slash computes to zero, and the lender withdraws their full credit.

**Exploit path:**

1. Market has `totalUnits = 1000`, `withdrawable = 600`, `lossFactor = 0`.
2. Lender A holds `credit = 100`. Borrower B has `debt = 200` with collateral worth only 100 → `badDebt = 100`.
3. Liquidator submits `liquidate(borrower=B, ...)` to the mempool.
4. Lender A sees the pending transaction and front-runs with `withdraw(units=100)`.
5. Inside `withdraw`, `_updatePosition` is called. At this moment `marketState[id].lossFactor` is still `0` and `_position.lastLossFactor` is also `0`, so `postSlashCredit = 100 * (MAX - 0) / (MAX - 0) = 100`. No slash.
6. `_position.credit -= 100`, `_marketState.withdrawable -= 100`. Lender A receives 100 loan tokens.
7. `liquidate` mines: `lossFactor` increases by `badDebt/totalUnits = 10%`. But Lender A is already out.
8. Remaining lenders (holding 900 units) absorb the full 100-unit bad debt — a ~11.1% loss instead of the expected 10%.

**Affected functions and lines:**

- `liquidate` — `lossFactor` update: [1](#0-0) 
- `withdraw` — calls `_updatePosition` before decrementing credit: [2](#0-1) 
- `updatePositionView` — slash computed against current (not future) `lossFactor`: [3](#0-2) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A lender who successfully front-runs avoids their entire proportional share of bad debt. The loss is redistributed to all remaining lenders, who each absorb a larger-than-expected slash. In a market where a large lender front-runs a large bad debt event, the remaining lenders could suffer materially amplified losses. This is a direct, quantifiable financial loss to honest lenders — not a theoretical concern.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The preconditions are realistic:

- **`withdrawable` must be ≥ lender's credit**: In any active market where some borrowers have repaid (via `repay`, which increments `withdrawable` at line 509), this condition is routinely met. Even partial front-runs (withdrawing up to available `withdrawable`) are profitable.
- **Mempool visibility**: On Ethereum mainnet and most EVM chains, pending transactions are publicly visible. Any lender running a node or watching a mempool service can detect a `liquidate` call targeting a borrower in their market.
- **No special privilege required**: The attacker is a normal lender using the standard `withdraw` function.
- **Gas auction**: The attacker simply submits `withdraw` with a higher gas price than the liquidator's transaction.

---

### Recommendation

Apply the `lossFactor` update **before** allowing any withdrawals in the same block, or — more robustly — snapshot and apply the slash atomically at the time of `liquidate` to all current credit holders. A simpler mitigation is to add a per-market "pending bad debt" flag that blocks `withdraw` until the bad debt liquidation is processed, similar to how `liquidationLocked` prevents liquidation of the seller during `take` callbacks.

Alternatively, consider computing the slash eagerly at `liquidate` time by iterating over lenders (though this is gas-prohibitive for many lenders) or by using a checkpoint mechanism where `withdraw` always applies the latest `lossFactor` **after** any pending bad debt in the same transaction is settled first.

### Proof of Concept

**Setup:**
- Market M with `loanToken = USDC`, single collateral.
- `totalUnits = 1000`, `withdrawable = 600`, `lossFactor = 0`.
- Lender A: `credit = 100`, `lastLossFactor = 0`.
- Borrower B: `debt = 200`, collateral value = 100 (undercollateralized, `badDebt = 100`).

**Steps:**

1. Liquidator calls `liquidate(market=M, borrower=B, seizedAssets=0, repaidUnits=0, ...)` — this will realize `badDebt = 100` and increase `lossFactor`.

2. Lender A observes this in the mempool and submits `withdraw(market=M, units=100, onBehalf=A, receiver=A)` with higher gas.

3. Lender A's `withdraw` mines first:
   - `_updatePosition` called: `lossFactor` still `0`, `lastLossFactor = 0` → `postSlashCredit = 100`. No slash.
   - `credit[A] = 0`, `withdrawable = 500`. Lender A receives 100 USDC.

4. Liquidator's `liquidate` mines:
   - `badDebt = 100`, `totalUnits = 900` (already decremented by Lender A's withdraw).
   - New `lossFactor = MAX - (MAX - 0) * (900 - 100) / 900 = MAX - MAX * 800/900 ≈ MAX * 100/900`.
   - Remaining 900 units of credit absorb the full 100-unit loss (~11.1% each instead of 10%).

**Expected (correct) behavior:** Lender A should have received `100 * (1 - 100/1000) = 90 USDC`.

**Actual behavior:** Lender A receives 100 USDC; remaining lenders absorb Lender A's 10-unit share of the loss. [4](#0-3) [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5)

    function _updatePosition(Market memory market, bytes32 id, address user)
        internal
        returns (uint128, uint128, uint128)
    {
        Position storage _position = position[id][user];
        (uint128 newCredit, uint128 newPendingFee, uint128 accruedFee) = updatePositionView(market, id, user);

        uint128 creditDecrease = _position.credit - newCredit;
        uint128 pendingFeeDecrease = _position.pendingFee - newPendingFee;

        _position.credit = newCredit;
        _position.lastLossFactor = marketState[id].lossFactor;
        _position.pendingFee = newPendingFee;
        _position.lastAccrual = uint128(block.timestamp);
        marketState[id].continuousFeeCredit += UtilsLib.toUint128(accruedFee);

        emit EventsLib.UpdatePosition(id, user, creditDecrease, pendingFeeDecrease, accruedFee);

        return (newCredit, newPendingFee, accruedFee);
    }
