Audit Report

## Title
Unconstrained `receiver` in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` and `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget` allows authorized caller to redirect victim's loan token proceeds - (`File: src/periphery/MidnightBundles.sol`)

## Summary
`MidnightBundles.supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` and `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget` accept a caller-controlled `receiver` address with no constraint that it equals `taker` or `msg.sender`. An attacker holding a Midnight authorization from the victim can supply their own collateral into the victim's position, force the victim to accrue debt by taking buy offers, and redirect all resulting loan token proceeds to themselves, leaving the victim with debt and no compensation.

## Finding Description

In `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` (lines 117–169), the sole authorization guard is:

```solidity
require(taker == msg.sender || IMidnight(MIDNIGHT).isAuthorized(taker, msg.sender), Unauthorized());
``` [1](#0-0) 

This confirms the caller is authorized to act on behalf of `taker`. It says nothing about where proceeds must go.

Collateral is pulled from `msg.sender` (attacker) and credited to `taker` (victim):

```solidity
pullToken(token, msg.sender, collateralSupplies[i].assets, collateralSupplies[i].permit);
...
IMidnight(MIDNIGHT).supplyCollateral(market, ..., taker);
``` [2](#0-1) 

`take` is called with `receiverIfTakerIsSeller = address(this)`, so the bundler accumulates all `sellerAssets`:

```solidity
.take(takes[i].offer, takes[i].ratifierData, unitsToTake, taker, address(this), address(0), "")
``` [3](#0-2) 

The final transfer sends all accumulated loan tokens to the raw, unchecked `receiver` parameter:

```solidity
SafeTransferLib.safeTransfer(loanToken, receiver, filledSellerAssets - referralFeeAssets);
``` [4](#0-3) 

**Exploit flow:**
1. Attacker calls `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget(targetUnits, 0, victim, attacker, ...)`.
2. Attacker's collateral is pulled from `msg.sender` and deposited into the victim's Midnight position.
3. The bundler takes buy offers on behalf of the victim, causing the victim to accrue debt equal to `targetUnits`.
4. All resulting loan tokens accumulate in the bundler and are forwarded to `receiver = attacker`.
5. Victim holds new debt with attacker-supplied collateral; attacker holds the loan tokens.
6. Attacker, still holding the victim's authorization, can subsequently call `withdrawCollateral` on behalf of the victim to remove the collateral they deposited, leaving the victim with uncollateralized debt subject to liquidation.

The identical flaw exists in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget`: [5](#0-4) 

## Impact Explanation

Direct theft of loan token proceeds: the victim accrues protocol debt and receives zero loan tokens. If the attacker subsequently withdraws the collateral they supplied (permitted by the same authorization), the victim's position becomes undercollateralized and subject to liquidation, resulting in total loss of any remaining collateral. This constitutes unauthorized movement of assets and forced insolvency of a user position — a critical in-scope impact.

## Likelihood Explanation

**Required preconditions:**
1. Victim has authorized `MidnightBundles` on Midnight — standard for any bundler user.
2. Victim has authorized the attacker on Midnight — realistic for keeper bots, co-signers, or UI-prompted delegations.
3. At least one buy offer exists in the market — normal market condition.

All three are achievable without any privileged protocol access. The attacker is an ordinary user holding a user-granted authorization, not a governance or admin key. The attack is repeatable as long as both authorizations remain active and buy offers exist. The attacker recovers their collateral cost via the stolen loan tokens, making the attack self-financing.

## Recommendation

Enforce that `receiver` must equal `taker` in both affected functions. The simplest fix:

```solidity
// In supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget and supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget
require(receiver == taker, InvalidReceiver());
```

Alternatively, if the design intent is to allow the caller to receive proceeds on their own behalf, enforce `receiver == msg.sender` and document that the caller bears the economic outcome. Either constraint closes the gap between authorization-to-act and authorization-to-receive.

## Proof of Concept

**Minimal manual steps:**

1. Deploy Midnight and MidnightBundles on a local fork.
2. Create accounts: `victim`, `attacker`.
3. `victim` calls `IMidnight.setAuthorization(address(bundles), true)` and `IMidnight.setAuthorization(attacker, true)`.
4. Create a buy offer in the market (any maker).
5. `attacker` calls `bundles.supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget(N, 0, victim, attacker, [collateralSupply], [take], 0, address(0))` with sufficient collateral approved.
6. Assert: `loanToken.balanceOf(attacker)` increased by `filledSellerAssets`; victim's Midnight debt increased by `N` units; victim received zero loan tokens.
7. `attacker` calls `IMidnight.withdrawCollateral(market, idx, amount, victim, attacker)` (authorized).
8. Assert: victim's collateral balance is zero; victim's debt remains; victim's position is undercollateralized.
