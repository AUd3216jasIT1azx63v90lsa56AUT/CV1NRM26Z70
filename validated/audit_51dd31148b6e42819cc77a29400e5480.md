### Title
Transaction Displacement Inflates Staker Entry Time, Reducing Bonus Share and Locking Withdrawal - (File: src/ConfidencePool.sol)

### Summary
`stake()` records `block.timestamp` as the deposit entry time with no deadline guard. A mempool-visible transaction can be displaced into a later block after `riskWindowStart` is sealed, causing the staker's entry time to be recorded as the delayed block timestamp rather than `riskWindowStart`. This permanently reduces the staker's k=2 bonus share and simultaneously locks them out of `withdraw()`.

### Finding Description
`stake()` computes the entry time as:

```solidity
uint256 newEntry = block.timestamp;
uint256 start = riskWindowStart;
if (start != 0 && newEntry < start) newEntry = start;
``` [1](#0-0) 

The clamping on line 250 only fires when `newEntry < start` — i.e., when the transaction executes *before* `riskWindowStart` was set, or in the same block it was set. If the transaction executes *after* `riskWindowStart` is already sealed (because it was displaced in the mempool), `newEntry = block.timestamp > riskWindowStart`, so no clamping occurs and the staker's entry time is the delayed timestamp.

A staker who submitted their transaction while the registry was still in a pre-risk state (intending to be a pre-risk depositor whose entry would be clamped to `riskWindowStart`) instead has their entry recorded at the later block timestamp. Their k=2 score is `amount × (T − T_delayed)²` rather than `amount × (T − riskWindowStart)²`. Because `T_delayed > riskWindowStart`, the score — and thus the bonus share — is strictly smaller.

Simultaneously, because `riskWindowStart != 0` when their delayed transaction executes, `withdraw()` is permanently disabled for them:

```solidity
if (
    riskWindowStart != 0
        || (state != IAttackRegistry.ContractState.NOT_DEPLOYED
            && state != IAttackRegistry.ContractState.NEW_DEPLOYMENT
            && state != IAttackRegistry.ContractState.ATTACK_REQUESTED)
) {
    revert WithdrawsDisabled();
}
``` [2](#0-1) 

The staker is locked in with a reduced bonus share they never consented to.

### Impact Explanation
A staker who intended to enter pre-risk (and expected their entry to be clamped to `riskWindowStart`, earning the full risk-window duration in the k=2 formula) instead has their entry time pushed forward by the displacement delay. Their bonus share is reduced proportionally to `(T − T_delayed)² / (T − riskWindowStart)²`. In addition, they lose the ability to exit via `withdraw()` — a right they would have retained had their transaction been included in the intended block. The bonus loss is redistributed to other stakers; no funds leave the protocol, but the victim suffers a material, irreversible reduction in their entitled payout.

### Likelihood Explanation
`stake()` transactions are publicly visible in the mempool. Any other staker (or MEV searcher acting on behalf of one) has a direct financial incentive to delay a competing staker's transaction past the `riskWindowStart` seal: doing so increases their own relative bonus share. The attacker only needs to (1) observe the pending `stake()` in the mempool, (2) call `pokeRiskWindow()` or submit their own `stake()` to seal `riskWindowStart`, and (3) front-run the victim's transaction with a higher-gas replacement to push it to a later block. All three steps are permissionless and require no privileged access. The attack is most effective in the block(s) immediately after the registry transitions to `UNDER_ATTACK`, when `riskWindowStart` is being sealed for the first time.

### Recommendation
Add a `deadline` parameter to `stake()` and revert if `block.timestamp > deadline`:

```solidity
function stake(uint256 amount, uint256 deadline) external nonReentrant whenPoolNotPaused {
    if (block.timestamp > deadline) revert DeadlineExpired();
    // ... rest of function
}
```

This mirrors the standard Uniswap/AMM pattern and lets stakers express the latest block at which their intended entry time is still acceptable. A displaced transaction that would record a materially later entry time simply reverts, allowing the staker to resubmit with updated parameters or choose not to stake.

### Proof of Concept

1. Pool is deployed; registry is in `NEW_DEPLOYMENT`. `riskWindowStart = 0`.
2. Alice submits `stake(1000e18)` at block timestamp `T0`. Her intent: enter pre-risk, be clamped to `riskWindowStart` when the window opens.
3. Registry transitions to `UNDER_ATTACK` at `T_attack > T0`.
4. Bob (a competing staker already in the pool) sees Alice's pending transaction in the mempool.
5. Bob calls `pokeRiskWindow()` at timestamp `T1 > T_attack`, sealing `riskWindowStart = T1`. [3](#0-2) 
6. Bob front-runs Alice's transaction with a higher-gas transaction, pushing Alice's tx to a later block at timestamp `T2 > T1`.
7. Alice's `stake()` executes: `newEntry = T2`, `start = T1`, `T2 > T1` → no clamping → `newEntry = T2`. [4](#0-3) 
8. At resolution with `T = riskWindowEnd`:
   - **Alice's actual score:** `1000e18 × (T − T2)²`
   - **Alice's expected score (pre-risk entry, clamped):** `1000e18 × (T − T1)²`
   - Since `T2 > T1`, Alice's bonus share is strictly less than she was entitled to.
9. Alice attempts `withdraw()` → reverts `WithdrawsDisabled` because `riskWindowStart != 0`. [2](#0-1) 
10. Alice is permanently locked in with a reduced bonus share she never agreed to accept.
