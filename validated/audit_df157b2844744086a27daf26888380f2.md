### Title
Lenders Can Front-Run Bad Debt Socialization to Escape `lossFactor` Slashing — (File: src/Midnight.sol)

---

### Summary

The `lossFactor` mechanism in Midnight is designed to socialize bad debt proportionally among all lenders in a market. However, because `lossFactor` is only updated inside `liquidate()` and `_updatePosition()` applies only the **already-committed** `lossFactor`, a lender can front-run a pending `liquidate()` call that would realize bad debt. By calling `withdraw()` before the liquidation lands, the lender exits at full credit value, shifting their share of the loss entirely onto remaining lenders.

---

### Finding Description

**Root cause**: `lossFactor` is a lazy, pull-based accounting variable. It is only written in `liquidate()` when bad debt is realized. The `_updatePosition()` function — called at the top of `withdraw()` — applies only the **current** (pre-liquidation) `lossFactor` to the lender's position. If the lender's `position.lastLossFactor` already equals `marketState.lossFactor`, `_updatePosition` is a no-op for credit reduction.

**Code path**:

`withdraw()` calls `_updatePosition()` first: [1](#0-0) 

`_updatePosition()` computes `postSlashCredit` using the **current** `lossFactor`: [2](#0-1) 

`lossFactor` is only updated inside `liquidate()` when bad debt is realized: [3](#0-2) 

**Exploit flow**:

1. Market state: Alice (100 credit), Bob (100 credit), `totalUnits = 200`, `withdrawable = 100` (from prior repayments), `lossFactor = L`.
2. A borrower has 50 units of bad debt. A liquidator submits `liquidate()`.
3. Alice (or an MEV bot) observes the pending liquidation in the mempool and front-runs with `withdraw(market, 100, alice, alice)`.
4. Inside Alice's `withdraw()`: `_updatePosition` reads `lossFactor = L` and Alice's `lastLossFactor = L` → no credit reduction. Alice withdraws 100 tokens. `withdrawable` drops to 0.
5. The liquidation executes: `lossFactor` increases to `L'`, `totalUnits` drops to 150. Bob's credit is now worth 50 (all 50 bad debt socialized to Bob alone).
6. `withdrawable = 0`; Bob cannot withdraw anything.

Without front-running, both Alice and Bob would have had their credit reduced to 75 each, and each would withdraw 50 from the 100 `withdrawable` pool.

---

### Impact Explanation

A sophisticated lender (or MEV searcher acting on their behalf) can escape 100% of their share of a bad debt socialization event. The loss is pushed entirely onto the remaining lenders who did not or could not front-run. In a market with a single remaining lender, that lender absorbs the full bad debt. This directly violates the protocol's stated invariant:

> "When a borrower's bad debt is realized, it is socialized among lenders in this market." [4](#0-3) 

The `Position.lastLossFactor` field exists precisely to track which bad debt events a lender has already been charged for: [5](#0-4) 

But it provides no protection against a lender exiting before the `lossFactor` is written.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- **No privileged access required**: any lender with credit in the market can execute this.
- **Mempool visibility**: on Ethereum mainnet, pending transactions are publicly visible. MEV bots routinely front-run liquidations.
- **Precondition `withdrawable > 0`**: this is the normal state of any active market where borrowers have partially repaid or been partially liquidated. It is not a rare edge case.
- **Incentive is proportional to bad debt size**: the larger the bad debt event, the larger the gain from front-running, making the attack more attractive precisely when it is most damaging.

---

### Recommendation

Apply the `lossFactor` slash **before** allowing a withdrawal, which `_updatePosition` already does — the gap is that `lossFactor` itself is not yet updated. Two complementary mitigations:

1. **Snapshot-based accounting**: record each lender's credit at the time they entered the market and compute their share of any bad debt at withdrawal time using the final `lossFactor`, regardless of when they exit. This is the structural fix.
2. **Withdrawal delay / lock period**: analogous to the RocketPool recommendation — introduce a withdrawal queue so that a lender who requests a withdrawal cannot receive funds until a delay has passed, giving time for any pending bad debt to be realized and applied. This prevents atomic front-running.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
State:
  Alice: credit=100, lastLossFactor=L
  Bob:   credit=100, lastLossFactor=L
  marketState: totalUnits=200, lossFactor=L, withdrawable=100

Step 1 — Liquidator submits liquidate() (badDebt=50) → pending in mempool

Step 2 — Alice front-runs: withdraw(market, 100, alice, alice)
  _updatePosition: postSlashCredit = 100 * (MAX-L)/(MAX-L) = 100  [no change]
  credit[alice] -= 100  → 0
  withdrawable  -= 100  → 0
  totalUnits    -= 100  → 100
  Alice receives 100 tokens ✓

Step 3 — Liquidation executes:
  badDebt = 50
  lossFactor updated to L' (higher)
  totalUnits -= 50  → 50
  withdrawable += repaidUnits (from liquidator, say 0 for pure bad-debt call)

Step 4 — Bob calls withdraw():
  _updatePosition: postSlashCredit = 100 * (MAX-L')/(MAX-L) = 50
  credit[bob] = 50
  withdrawable = 0  → Bob cannot withdraw anything

Result:
  Alice received 100 (should have received 75)
  Bob received   0  (should have received 25)
  Alice's gain: +25 tokens at Bob's expense
```

    function withdraw(Market memory market, uint256 units, address onBehalf, address receiver) external {
        require(onBehalf == msg.sender || isAuthorized[onBehalf][msg.sender], Unauthorized());
        bytes32 id = touchMarket(market);
        MarketState storage _marketState = marketState[id];
        _updatePosition(market, id, onBehalf);

        Position storage _position = position[id][onBehalf];
        uint128 pendingFeeDecrease;
        if (_position.credit > 0) {
            pendingFeeDecrease = UtilsLib.toUint128(_position.pendingFee.mulDivUp(units, _position.credit));
            _position.pendingFee -= pendingFeeDecrease;
        }
        _position.credit -= UtilsLib.toUint128(units);
        _marketState.withdrawable -= UtilsLib.toUint128(units);
        _marketState.totalUnits -= UtilsLib.toUint128(units);

        emit EventsLib.Withdraw(msg.sender, id, units, onBehalf, receiver, pendingFeeDecrease);

        SafeTransferLib.safeTransfer(market.loanToken, receiver, units);
    }
