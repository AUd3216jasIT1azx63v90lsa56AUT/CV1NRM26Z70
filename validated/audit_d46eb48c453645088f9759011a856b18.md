Audit Report

## Title
Unconstrained `receiver` in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` and `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget` allows authorized caller to redirect victim's loan token proceeds - (File: src/periphery/MidnightBundles.sol)

## Summary
Both `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` and `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget` accept a caller-controlled `receiver` address with no constraint that it equals `taker` or `msg.sender`. An attacker holding a Midnight authorization from the victim can supply their own collateral into the victim's position, force the victim to accrue debt by taking buy offers, and redirect all resulting loan token proceeds to themselves, leaving the victim with debt and no compensation.

## Finding Description

The authorization guard at line 127 only confirms `msg.sender` may act on behalf of `taker`; it says nothing about where proceeds must flow:

```solidity
require(taker == msg.sender || IMidnight(MIDNIGHT).isAuthorized(taker, msg.sender), Unauthorized());
``` [1](#0-0) 

Collateral is pulled from `msg.sender` (attacker) and credited to `taker` (victim): [2](#0-1) 

`take` is called with `receiverIfTakerIsSeller = address(this)`, so the bundler accumulates all `sellerAssets`: [3](#0-2) 

The final transfer sends all accumulated loan tokens to the raw, unchecked `receiver` parameter: [4](#0-3) 

The identical pattern exists in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget`: [5](#0-4) 

**Exploit flow:**
1. Attacker calls `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget(targetUnits, 0, victim, attacker, ...)`.
2. Attacker's collateral is pulled from `msg.sender` and deposited into the victim's Midnight position.
3. The bundler takes buy offers on behalf of the victim (`taker`), causing the victim to accrue debt equal to `targetUnits`.
4. All resulting loan tokens accumulate in the bundler and are forwarded to `receiver = attacker`.
5. Victim holds new debt with attacker-supplied collateral; attacker holds the loan tokens.
6. Attacker, still holding the victim's authorization, calls `withdrawCollateral` on behalf of the victim to remove the collateral they deposited, leaving the victim with uncollateralized debt subject to liquidation.

No existing check prevents this. The `minSellerAssets` slippage guard (line 166) only ensures a minimum amount is transferred — it does not constrain *who* receives it. [6](#0-5) 

Contrast with the buy-side functions, where the refund is hardcoded to `msg.sender` (line 104), not a caller-supplied address — demonstrating the asymmetry is not intentional design: [7](#0-6) 

## Impact Explanation

Direct theft of loan token proceeds: the victim accrues protocol debt and receives zero loan tokens. If the attacker subsequently withdraws the collateral they supplied (permitted by the same authorization), the victim's position becomes undercollateralized and subject to liquidation, resulting in total loss of any remaining collateral. This constitutes unauthorized movement of assets and forced insolvency of a user position.

## Likelihood Explanation

**Required preconditions:**
1. Victim has authorized `MidnightBundles` on Midnight — standard for any bundler user.
2. Victim has authorized the attacker on Midnight — realistic for keeper bots, co-signers, or UI-prompted delegations.
3. At least one buy offer exists in the market — normal market condition.

All three are achievable without any privileged protocol access. The attacker is an ordinary user holding a user-granted authorization, not a governance or admin key. The attack is repeatable as long as both authorizations remain active and buy offers exist. The attacker recovers their collateral cost via the stolen loan tokens, making the attack self-financing.

## Recommendation

Constrain `receiver` to either `taker` or `msg.sender` at the top of both functions:

```solidity
require(receiver == taker || receiver == msg.sender, InvalidReceiver());
```

This mirrors the implicit constraint already present in the buy-side functions, where the refund is hardcoded to `msg.sender`.

## Proof of Concept

**Minimal manual steps:**

1. Deploy Midnight and `MidnightBundles` on a fork.
2. Create a market with a buy offer from `lender` at `MAX_TICK`.
3. `victim` calls `midnight.setIsAuthorized(attacker, true, victim)` and `midnight.setIsAuthorized(address(bundles), true, victim)`.
4. `attacker` approves `MidnightBundles` to spend collateral token.
5. `attacker` calls `bundles.supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget(targetUnits, 0, victim, attacker, collateralSupplies, takes, 0, address(0))`.
6. Assert: `midnight.debtOf(id, victim) == targetUnits` (victim has debt).
7. Assert: `loanToken.balanceOf(attacker) > 0` (attacker received loan tokens).
8. Assert: `loanToken.balanceOf(victim) == 0` (victim received nothing).
9. `attacker` calls `midnight.withdrawCollateral(market, 0, collateralAmount, victim, attacker)` — succeeds because attacker is still authorized.
10. Assert: victim's position is now undercollateralized and subject to liquidation.
