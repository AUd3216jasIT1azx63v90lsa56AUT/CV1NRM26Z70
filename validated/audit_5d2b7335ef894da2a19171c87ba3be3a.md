### Title
Sponsor can redirect all staker funds to themselves by updating `recoveryAddress` after `flagOutcome` — (File: src/ConfidencePool.sol)

### Summary
`setRecoveryAddress` imposes no lock once an outcome is flagged. In the bad-faith CORRUPTED path the entire pool (stakers' principal + bonus) is swept to `recoveryAddress` via the permissionless `claimCorrupted`. A malicious sponsor can call `setRecoveryAddress(sponsorWallet)` in the window between `flagOutcome` and `claimCorrupted`, redirecting 100 % of staker funds to themselves.

### Finding Description
`setRecoveryAddress` is gated only by `onlyOwner` (the sponsor) with no guard on `outcome`, `claimsStarted`, or any post-flag state:

```solidity
function setRecoveryAddress(address newRecoveryAddress) external onlyOwner {
    if (newRecoveryAddress == address(0)) revert InvalidRecoveryAddress();
    recoveryAddress = newRecoveryAddress;
    ...
}
``` [1](#0-0) 

`claimCorrupted` then sweeps the full live balance unconditionally to whatever `recoveryAddress` holds at call time:

```solidity
stakeToken.safeTransfer(recoveryAddress, toSweep);
``` [2](#0-1) 

Compare the documented temporal locks on the sponsor's other controls: `expiry` is locked after the first stake (`expiryLocked`), and scope is locked once the registry leaves pre-attack staging (`scopeLocked`). [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3)  No equivalent one-way latch exists for `recoveryAddress`. The README and DESIGN.md §10 list the sponsor's control over `recoveryAddress` without any temporal qualifier, unlike the explicit "only until the first stake" and "only until the registry leaves pre-attack staging" qualifiers for `expiry` and scope. [5](#0-4) 

`sweepUnclaimedCorrupted` (good-faith path, post-bounty-window) and `sweepUnclaimedBonus` (SURVIVED/EXPIRED) also transfer to `recoveryAddress`, so the same post-flag redirect applies to those flows. [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6) 

### Impact Explanation
In the bad-faith CORRUPTED path, `corruptedReserve` is set to `snapshotTotalStaked + snapshotTotalBonus` — the entire pool — and `claimCorrupted` sweeps the full live balance to `recoveryAddress`. [8](#0-7)  A sponsor who redirects `recoveryAddress` to their own wallet before `claimCorrupted` is called captures 100 % of all staker principal and bonus. Stakers have no recourse: `withdraw` is permanently disabled once `riskWindowStart != 0`, and the claim paths require a non-CORRUPTED outcome. [9](#0-8) 

### Likelihood Explanation
The moderator flags bad-faith CORRUPTED precisely when the agreement has been corrupted — the scenario where the sponsor is most likely to be adversarial. The attack requires only a single `setRecoveryAddress` call between `flagOutcome` and the first `claimCorrupted` call. `claimCorrupted` is permissionless, so the sponsor can also self-trigger it immediately after redirecting, collapsing the window to a single transaction. The sponsor is not listed as a trusted actor (unlike the moderator/DAO and factory owner), so this is within the adversarial model.

### Recommendation
Lock `recoveryAddress` changes once the outcome is no longer `UNRESOLVED`. Add a guard at the top of `setRecoveryAddress`:

```solidity
if (outcome != PoolStates.Outcome.UNRESOLVED) revert OutcomeAlreadySet();
```

This mirrors the pattern used for `expiry` (`expiryLocked`) and scope (`scopeLocked`) and is consistent with the documented principle that staker-relevant parameters should be verifiable at deposit time and immutable once resolution begins.

### Proof of Concept

1. Stakers deposit into the pool; `riskWindowStart` is sealed (withdrawals permanently disabled).
2. The agreement is corrupted; the registry transitions to `CORRUPTED`.
3. The honest moderator calls `flagOutcome(CORRUPTED, false, address(0))` (bad-faith). `snapshotTotalStaked + snapshotTotalBonus` is recorded as `corruptedReserve`. [10](#0-9) 
4. The sponsor calls `setRecoveryAddress(sponsorOwnWallet)` — succeeds with no guard. [1](#0-0) 
5. The sponsor (or anyone) calls `claimCorrupted()`. The full pool balance is transferred to `sponsorOwnWallet`. [11](#0-10) 
6. All stakers lose 100 % of their principal and bonus with no recovery path.
