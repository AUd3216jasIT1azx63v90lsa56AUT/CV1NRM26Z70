### Title
Borrowers Cannot Realize Full Loan Value Without Risking Post-Maturity Liquidation — (File: src/Midnight.sol)

---

### Summary

In Midnight, there is no grace period after market maturity. As soon as `block.timestamp > market.maturity`, any borrower with outstanding debt — including healthy, over-collateralized ones — can be liquidated in post-maturity mode. With `TIME_TO_MAX_LIF = 15 minutes`, the liquidation incentive factor (LIF) ramps from 1× to `maxLif` in just 15 minutes, creating a strong MEV incentive to front-run borrowers who attempt to repay at or near maturity.

---

### Finding Description

**Vulnerability class:** State transition / timing (no grace period at maturity boundary)

The `liquidate()` function in `src/Midnight.sol` enables post-maturity liquidation the instant `block.timestamp > market.maturity`, with no buffer:

```solidity
require(
    !liquidationLocked(id, borrower)
        && (postMaturityMode ? block.timestamp > market.maturity : originalDebt > maxDebt),
    NotLiquidatable()
);
``` [1](#0-0) 

In post-maturity mode, the Recovery Close Factor (RCF) is **deactivated**, meaning the liquidator can liquidate the borrower's entire position in one call. The LIF is computed as:

```solidity
uint256 lif = postMaturityMode
    ? UtilsLib.min(_maxLif, WAD + (_maxLif - WAD) * (block.timestamp - market.maturity) / TIME_TO_MAX_LIF)
    : _maxLif;
``` [2](#0-1) 

`TIME_TO_MAX_LIF` is hardcoded to **15 minutes**: [3](#0-2) 

For the lowest allowed LLTV tier (`LLTV_0 = 0.385`) with `LIQUIDATION_CURSOR_HIGH = 0.5`:

```
maxLif = WAD / (WAD - 0.5 * (WAD - 0.385)) ≈ 1.444e18  (44.4% discount)
``` [4](#0-3) 

The `repay()` function has **no post-maturity restriction** — borrowers can repay after maturity — but they race against liquidators: [5](#0-4) 

Critically, post-maturity liquidation applies to **any borrower with debt**, regardless of health. A healthy, over-collateralized borrower who submits a repayment transaction at maturity can be front-run by a liquidator calling `liquidate(..., postMaturityMode=true, ...)` in the same block.

---

### Impact Explanation

A borrower who holds their position to full term (the rational strategy to maximize loan value) becomes immediately exposed to collateral seizure the moment maturity passes. The liquidator profits:

```
profit = repaidUnits × (lif − 1)
```

For a $500K debt position with LLTV = 0.385 (maxLif ≈ 1.444):

| Time after maturity | LIF | Liquidator profit on $500K debt |
|---|---|---|
| +1 block (~12s) | ≈ 1.0059 | ≈ $2,960 |
| +1 minute | ≈ 1.030 | ≈ $14,800 |
| +15 minutes | 1.444 | ≈ $222,000 |

The borrower loses collateral worth far more than their debt, even though they were healthy and intended to repay. The RCF being deactivated in post-maturity mode means the entire position can be seized in a single transaction. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- MEV bots continuously monitor mempools for profitable liquidation opportunities. A $2,960 profit at maturity + 1 block is well above typical gas costs on Ethereum.
- The 15-minute ramp to maxLif means the incentive grows rapidly; bots have strong reason to front-run any repayment transaction submitted near maturity.
- No privileged access is required. Any external address can call `liquidate()` with `postMaturityMode = true` once `block.timestamp > market.maturity`.
- Borrowers who rationally maximize loan duration (holding to maturity) are the most exposed, making this a systematic risk for the protocol's user base.

---

### Recommendation

**Short term:** Introduce a grace period (e.g., 15–30 minutes) after `market.maturity` during which post-maturity liquidation is blocked. Change the condition from:

```solidity
postMaturityMode ? block.timestamp > market.maturity : originalDebt > maxDebt
```

to:

```solidity
postMaturityMode ? block.timestamp > market.maturity + GRACE_PERIOD : originalDebt > maxDebt
```

This gives borrowers a window to repay without being front-run, while still allowing liquidation of genuinely non-repaying borrowers.

**Long term:** Consider whether `TIME_TO_MAX_LIF = 15 minutes` is appropriate. A longer ramp reduces the immediate MEV incentive at maturity and gives borrowers more time to react.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice borrows in a Midnight market with `LLTV = 0.385`, `maxLif ≈ 1.444`, depositing $1M in collateral against a $500K debt.
2. Alice holds the position to full term to maximize her benefit-to-cost ratio.
3. At `block.timestamp = market.maturity + 1`, Alice submits a `repay()` transaction.
4. Eve (MEV bot) observes Alice's pending transaction and front-runs it by calling:
   ```solidity
   liquidate(market, collateralIndex, 0, 500_000e18, alice, true, eve, address(0), "")
   ```
   with `postMaturityMode = true`.
5. The `liquidate()` check passes: `block.timestamp > market.maturity` ✓, `_position.debt > 0` ✓, RCF deactivated in post-maturity mode ✓.
6. Eve repays Alice's $500K debt and seizes collateral worth $500K × 1.0059 ≈ $502,960, profiting ~$2,960 in the first block — growing to $222,000 if Alice's repayment is delayed 15 minutes.
7. Alice's repayment transaction reverts (debt already zero), and she has lost her collateral. [7](#0-6) [3](#0-2)

    function liquidate(
        Market calldata market,
        uint256 collateralIndex,
        uint256 seizedAssets,
        uint256 repaidUnits,
        address borrower,
        bool postMaturityMode,
        address receiver,
        address callback,
        bytes calldata data
    ) external returns (uint256, uint256) {
        bytes32 id = touchMarket(market);
        MarketState storage _marketState = marketState[id];
        Position storage _position = position[id][borrower];
        require(UtilsLib.atMostOneNonZero(repaidUnits, seizedAssets), InconsistentInput());
        require(_position.debt > 0, NotBorrower()); // to avoid no-op liquidations of non borrower positions.
        require(
            market.liquidatorGate == address(0) || ILiquidatorGate(market.liquidatorGate).canLiquidate(msg.sender),
            LiquidatorGatedFromLiquidating()
        );

        uint256 maxDebt;
        uint256 liquidatedCollatPrice;
        uint256 originalDebt = _position.debt;
        uint256 badDebt = originalDebt;
        uint128 _collateralBitmap = _position.collateralBitmap;
        while (_collateralBitmap != 0) {
            uint256 i = UtilsLib.msb(_collateralBitmap);
            CollateralParams memory _collateralParam = market.collateralParams[i];
            uint256 price = IOracle(_collateralParam.oracle).price();
            if (i == collateralIndex) liquidatedCollatPrice = price;
            uint256 _collateral = _position.collateral[i];
            maxDebt += _collateral.mulDivDown(price, ORACLE_PRICE_SCALE).mulDivDown(_collateralParam.lltv, WAD);
            badDebt = badDebt.zeroFloorSub(
                _collateral.mulDivUp(price, ORACLE_PRICE_SCALE).mulDivUp(WAD, _collateralParam.maxLif)
            );
            _collateralBitmap = _collateralBitmap.clearBit(i);
        }

        require(
            !liquidationLocked(id, borrower)
                && (postMaturityMode ? block.timestamp > market.maturity : originalDebt > maxDebt),
            NotLiquidatable()
        );
