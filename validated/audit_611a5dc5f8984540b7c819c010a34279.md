Audit Report

## Title
Unconstrained `receiver` in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` and `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget` allows authorized caller to redirect victim's sellerAssets - (`src/periphery/MidnightBundles.sol`)

## Summary
`MidnightBundles.supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` and `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget` accept a caller-controlled `receiver` address with no constraint that it equals `taker` or `msg.sender`. An attacker holding a Midnight authorization from the victim can supply their own collateral into the victim's position, force the victim to accrue debt by taking buy offers on the victim's behalf, and redirect all resulting `sellerAssets` (loan tokens) to themselves. The victim is left with unwanted debt and collateral they did not choose to acquire.

## Finding Description

**Root cause:** The `receiver` parameter in both sell-side bundle functions is never validated against `taker` or `msg.sender`.

In `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` (lines 117–169): [1](#0-0) 

This is the sole authorization guard. It confirms `msg.sender` is authorized by `taker` but says nothing about where proceeds must go. [2](#0-1) 

Collateral is pulled from `msg.sender` (attacker) and credited to `taker` (victim). [3](#0-2) 

`receiverIfTakerIsSeller = address(this)` routes all `sellerAssets` to the bundler contract.

In `Midnight.take`, when `offer.buy == true`: [4](#0-3) [5](#0-4) 

`sellerAssets` are transferred from the payer directly to `address(this)` (the bundler). The bundler then forwards them: [6](#0-5) 

`receiver` is the raw attacker-supplied parameter — never checked against `taker`.

The identical flaw exists in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget`: [7](#0-6) 

**Exploit flow:**
1. Attacker calls `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget(targetUnits, 0, victim, attacker, [attackerCollateral], [buyOffers], 0, address(0))`.
2. Line 127 passes: attacker is authorized by victim.
3. Attacker's collateral is pulled from `msg.sender` and supplied to victim's position (lines 134–139).
4. `Midnight.take` is called with `taker=victim`, `receiverIfTakerIsSeller=address(this)`. Victim accrues debt; bundler receives `sellerAssets`.
5. Line 168 transfers `filledSellerAssets` to `receiver = attacker`.

**Why existing checks fail:** The `isAuthorized` check at line 127 and the `isAuthorized[taker][msg.sender]` check inside `Midnight.take` (line 346) both verify delegation rights only — neither constrains the destination of proceeds. [8](#0-7) 

## Impact Explanation
Direct theft of assets: the victim accrues `targetUnits` of debt with zero loan tokens received, while the attacker receives the full `filledSellerAssets`. The victim's position is collateralized by attacker-supplied collateral; the attacker has effectively liquidated their own collateral position through the victim, extracting loan tokens at the victim's expense. This is an unauthorized movement of assets caused by a broken authorization boundary in the bundler — a critical in-scope impact.

## Likelihood Explanation
Three preconditions are required:
1. Victim has authorized `MidnightBundles` on Midnight — required for any bundler user.
2. Victim has authorized the attacker on Midnight — realistic for keeper bots, co-signers, or UI-prompted authorizations.
3. At least one buy offer exists in the market — normal market condition.

All three are realistic in normal protocol usage. The attack is repeatable as long as both authorizations remain active and buy offers exist. The attacker needs only enough collateral tokens to supply (which they effectively convert to loan tokens via the attack).

## Recommendation
Enforce that `receiver` equals `taker` in both functions, since the proceeds of a sell executed on behalf of `taker` belong to `taker`:

```solidity
// In supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget and supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget
require(receiver == taker, InvalidReceiver());
```

Alternatively, remove the `receiver` parameter entirely and always transfer to `taker`.

## Proof of Concept

**Minimal unit test steps:**
1. Deploy `Midnight` and `MidnightBundles`.
2. Create a market with a loan token and collateral token.
3. Have `maker` post a buy offer (e.g., `offer.buy = true`).
4. `victim` calls `Midnight.setAuthorization(address(bundles), true)` and `Midnight.setAuthorization(attacker, true)`.
5. Attacker approves `MidnightBundles` to spend their collateral tokens.
6. Attacker calls:
   ```solidity
   bundles.supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget(
       targetUnits,
       0,           // minSellerAssets
       victim,      // taker
       attacker,    // receiver ← attacker-controlled
       [CollateralSupply(0, collateralAmount, emptyPermit)],
       [Take(buyOffer, "", targetUnits)],
       0, address(0)
   );
   ```
7. Assert: `loanToken.balanceOf(attacker) == filledSellerAssets` (attacker received loan tokens).
8. Assert: `Midnight.position(id, victim).debt > 0` (victim has debt).
9. Assert: `loanToken.balanceOf(victim) == 0` (victim received nothing).
