### Title
Validator Timestamp Manipulation Inflates Post-Maturity LIF, Enabling Over-Seizure of Collateral — (File: src/Midnight.sol)

---

### Summary

`Midnight.sol` computes the post-maturity Liquidation Incentive Factor (LIF) using `block.timestamp` over a ramp window of only `TIME_TO_MAX_LIF = 15 minutes`. A validator who is also a liquidator can manipulate `block.timestamp` by up to ~12 seconds within their own block, shifting the LIF upward by up to 1.33% of its full range. This allows the attacker to seize more collateral than they are entitled to for a given repaid debt amount.

---

### Finding Description

**Vulnerability class**: Pricing/financial manipulation via timestamp dependence.

**Root cause**: In `liquidate()`, the post-maturity LIF is computed as:

```solidity
lif = UtilsLib.min(_maxLif, WAD + (_maxLif - WAD) * (block.timestamp - market.maturity) / TIME_TO_MAX_LIF)
``` [1](#0-0) 

`TIME_TO_MAX_LIF` is defined as exactly **15 minutes (900 seconds)**: [2](#0-1) 

On Ethereum PoS, a validator controls the `block.timestamp` of their own proposed block. The timestamp must be strictly greater than the parent's timestamp and within a reasonable bound (~12 seconds per slot). This means a validator-liquidator can advance `block.timestamp` by up to ~12 seconds relative to the honest value.

**Exploit path**:

1. A post-maturity liquidation opportunity exists (borrower has not repaid after `market.maturity`).
2. The attacker is a validator and proposes the block containing their own `liquidate()` call.
3. The attacker sets `block.timestamp` to be ~12 seconds higher than the honest value.
4. `(block.timestamp - market.maturity)` increases by 12 seconds.
5. `lif` increases by `(_maxLif - WAD) * 12 / 900`.
6. When `seizedAssets > 0` is the input, `repaidUnits` is computed as:

```solidity
repaidUnits = seizedAssets.mulDivUp(liquidatedCollatPrice, ORACLE_PRICE_SCALE).mulDivUp(WAD, lif);
``` [3](#0-2) 

A higher `lif` means fewer `repaidUnits` are required to seize the same `seizedAssets` — the attacker repays less debt while taking the same collateral.

Alternatively, when `repaidUnits > 0` is the input:

```solidity
seizedAssets = repaidUnits.mulDivDown(lif, WAD).mulDivDown(ORACLE_PRICE_SCALE, liquidatedCollatPrice);
``` [4](#0-3) 

A higher `lif` means more `seizedAssets` are returned for the same `repaidUnits`.

In post-maturity mode, the RCF is deactivated, so there is no cap on the liquidation amount: [5](#0-4)

### Impact Explanation

**Concrete financial impact**:

For LLTV = 0.385 with `LIQUIDATION_CURSOR_HIGH = 0.5`:

```
maxLif = WAD / (WAD - 0.5 * (WAD - 0.385 * WAD))
       = 1e18 / (1e18 - 0.5 * 0.615e18)
       ≈ 1.4440e18
``` [6](#0-5) 

LIF range = `maxLif - WAD ≈ 0.4440e18`.

With 12 seconds of manipulation:
- `Δlif = 0.4440e18 * 12 / 900 ≈ 0.005920e18`

For a $10M collateral position:
- Extra collateral seized ≈ `$10M * 0.00592 ≈ $59,200` per liquidation event.

The borrower's position is drained of more collateral than the protocol intends, with the excess going to the attacker-liquidator. The loss is borne by the borrower.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Attacker requirements**:
- Must be an Ethereum validator (requires 32 ETH stake, ~$100k at current prices — permissionless, not a privileged protocol role).
- Must have a post-maturity liquidation opportunity available in their proposed block.
- No other protocol permissions needed.

**Feasibility**: Validator-MEV is a well-established attack vector. Liquidation bots routinely monitor for liquidatable positions. A validator running a liquidation bot can trivially combine both roles. The attack is silent, leaves no on-chain evidence of manipulation, and is repeatable across every block the attacker proposes.

---

### Recommendation

Replace the `block.timestamp`-based LIF ramp with a `block.number`-based equivalent, or increase `TIME_TO_MAX_LIF` to a value where 12 seconds of manipulation is economically negligible (e.g., 7 days instead of 15 minutes reduces the per-second impact by ~672×). Alternatively, snap `block.timestamp` to the nearest slot boundary before use in the LIF formula to limit the manipulation surface.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup**:
- Market: `loanToken = USDC`, `lltv = 0.385e18`, `maxLif = 1.444e18`, `maturity = T`
- Borrower: `debt = 5,000,000 units`, `collateral = $10M worth`
- Attacker: validator + liquidator

**Steps**:

1. Market matures at timestamp `T`. Borrower does not repay.
2. Attacker waits until they are the next block proposer.
3. Attacker sets `block.timestamp = T + 12 + 12` (honest value would be `T + 12`).
4. Attacker calls `liquidate(..., seizedAssets = 10_000_000e6, repaidUnits = 0, postMaturityMode = true, ...)`.
5. Protocol computes:
   - Honest `lif = WAD + 0.444e18 * 12 / 900 = 1.005920e18`
   - Manipulated `lif = WAD + 0.444e18 * 24 / 900 = 1.011840e18`
6. Honest `repaidUnits = seizedAssets * price / lif_honest`
   Manipulated `repaidUnits = seizedAssets * price / lif_manipulated` → attacker pays ~0.59% less debt to seize the same $10M collateral.
7. **Net gain**: ~$59,200 extracted from the borrower per liquidation event, with no protocol-level check to detect or prevent it. [7](#0-6) [2](#0-1)
