Audit Report

## Title
Uncaught `buyerAssetsToUnits` Arithmetic Underflow via tick=0 Buy Offer DoSes Bundle Sell Flows - (File: src/periphery/ConsumableUnitsLib.sol)

## Summary
`TickLib.tickToPrice(0)` returns exactly `0`. A maker can publish a buy offer at `tick=0` with `maxUnits=0` and `maxAssets>0`, causing `ConsumableUnitsLib.consumableUnits` to call `TakeAmountsLib.buyerAssetsToUnits`, which performs the checked subtraction `offerPrice - settlementFee` (0 minus a positive value), reverting under Solidity 0.8 arithmetic. Because `consumableUnits` is invoked **outside** the `try/catch` block in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` and `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget`, the revert propagates and aborts the entire bundle transaction.

## Finding Description

**`tickToPrice(0) == 0` is proven by the test suite:** [1](#0-0) 

**`consumableUnits` routes into `buyerAssetsToUnits` when `offer.maxUnits == 0` and `offer.buy == true`:** [2](#0-1) 

**`buyerAssetsToUnits` performs the unchecked subtraction at line 26:** [3](#0-2) 

With `offerPrice = 0` and any `settlementFee > 0`, `offerPrice - settlementFee` underflows under Solidity 0.8 checked arithmetic and reverts.

**`consumableUnits` is called before the `try` block in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget`:** [4](#0-3) 

**Same pattern in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget` — both `sellerAssetsToUnits` (line 286) and `consumableUnits` (line 290) are outside the `try`:** [5](#0-4) 

**The NatSpec explicitly acknowledges this gap:** [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6) 

The `Midnight.take` guard against `offerPrice < settlementFee` (inside the `try`) is irrelevant here because `consumableUnits` fires first and its revert is never caught.

## Impact Explanation
Any call to `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` or `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget` that includes the crafted buy offer in its `takes` array reverts entirely. Legitimate borrowers (takers) who attempt to sell units against a bundle of buy offers are completely blocked whenever the attacker's offer appears in the bundle. This is a targeted, repeatable DoS on core bundle sell functionality with no capital cost to the attacker.

## Likelihood Explanation
All preconditions are trivially achievable by any unprivileged maker: `offer.buy = true`, `offer.tick = 0` (valid since `0 % tickSpacing == 0` for any spacing), `offer.maxUnits = 0`, `offer.maxAssets > 0`. Every market with a non-zero settlement fee (the default) satisfies `settlementFee > 0`. The attacker risks no capital because the offer itself would also revert inside `Midnight.take`, but its mere presence in a bundle is sufficient to abort the whole transaction. The attack is repeatable at gas cost only.

## Recommendation
Treat a revert from `consumableUnits` (and the `TakeAmountsLib` calls outside the `try`) the same way a revert from `take` is treated — skip the offer. The cleanest fix is to move the `unitsToTake` computation inside the `try` block or wrap it in its own `try/catch`, returning 0 on revert (which causes `min(...)` to yield 0 and the offer to be skipped). Alternatively, add a guard in `buyerAssetsToUnits` and `sellerAssetsToUnits` to return 0 instead of reverting when `offerPrice < settlementFee`, consistent with the skip-on-failure philosophy already applied to `take`.

## Proof of Concept
1. Deploy on a fork with a market where `settlementFee > 0`.
2. Attacker calls `Midnight.makeOffer` with `buy=true`, `tick=0`, `maxUnits=0`, `maxAssets=1`.
3. A legitimate taker calls `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` with a `takes` array that includes the attacker's offer alongside valid offers.
4. The transaction reverts at the `ConsumableUnitsLib.consumableUnits` call (line 150) due to arithmetic underflow in `buyerAssetsToUnits` line 26 (`0 - settlementFee`).
5. Confirm: removing the attacker's offer from the array allows the transaction to succeed.
