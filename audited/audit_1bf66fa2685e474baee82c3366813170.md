Audit Report

## Title
Uncaught `buyerAssetsToUnits` / `sellerAssetsToUnits` Arithmetic Underflow via `tick=0` Buy Offer DoSes Bundle Sell Flows - (File: src/periphery/ConsumableUnitsLib.sol)

## Summary
`TickLib.tickToPrice(0)` returns exactly `0`, as proven by the test suite. A maker can post a buy offer with `tick=0`, `maxUnits=0`, and `buy=true`. When such an offer appears in the `takes` array of `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` or `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget`, the call to `ConsumableUnitsLib.consumableUnits` (or `TakeAmountsLib.sellerAssetsToUnits`) executes the checked subtraction `offerPrice - settlementFee` = `0 - positive`, which underflows under Solidity 0.8 and reverts. Because these calls occur **outside** the `try/catch` block, the revert propagates and aborts the entire bundle transaction.

## Finding Description

**Root cause chain:**

1. `TickLib.tickToPrice(0) == 0` is confirmed by the test suite: [1](#0-0) 

2. `ConsumableUnitsLib.consumableUnits` routes into `TakeAmountsLib.buyerAssetsToUnits` when `offer.maxUnits == 0` and `offer.buy == true`: [2](#0-1) 

3. Inside `buyerAssetsToUnits`, line 26 performs the checked subtraction `offerPrice - settlementFee`. With `offerPrice = 0` and any `settlementFee > 0`, this underflows and reverts: [3](#0-2) 

4. The same underflow exists in `sellerAssetsToUnits` at line 44, which is called directly in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget`: [4](#0-3) 

5. In `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget`, `ConsumableUnitsLib.consumableUnits` is called at line 150 inside the `min(...)` expression, which is **before** the `try` block at line 152. The revert is not caught: [5](#0-4) 

6. In `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget`, both `TakeAmountsLib.sellerAssetsToUnits` (line 286) and `ConsumableUnitsLib.consumableUnits` (line 290) are outside the `try` block at line 292: [6](#0-5) 

7. The NatSpec explicitly acknowledges this gap — these functions are documented to revert if `ConsumableUnitsLib` or `TakeAmountsLib` reverts, confirming the revert is not caught: [7](#0-6) [8](#0-7) 

The guard inside `Midnight.take` against `offerPrice < settlementFee` is irrelevant because `consumableUnits` / `sellerAssetsToUnits` fire and revert before `take` is ever called.

## Impact Explanation
Any call to `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` or `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget` that includes the crafted buy offer anywhere in its `takes` array reverts entirely. Legitimate borrowers (takers) attempting to sell units against a bundle of buy offers are completely blocked whenever the attacker's offer appears in the bundle. This is a targeted, repeatable DoS on core bundle sell functionality with no capital cost to the attacker.

## Likelihood Explanation
All preconditions are trivially achievable by any unprivileged maker: `offer.buy = true`, `offer.tick = 0` (valid since `0 <= MAX_TICK` and `0 % tickSpacing == 0` for any spacing), `offer.maxUnits = 0`. Every market with a non-zero settlement fee satisfies `settlementFee > 0`. The attacker risks no capital because the offer itself would also revert inside `Midnight.take`, but its mere presence in a bundle is sufficient to abort the whole transaction. The attack is repeatable at gas cost only.

## Recommendation
Move the `consumableUnits` (and `sellerAssetsToUnits` / `buyerAssetsToUnits`) calls inside the `try/catch` block, or add a pre-check guard that skips offers where `tickToPrice(offer.tick) < settlementFee` before invoking `TakeAmountsLib`. Alternatively, wrap the entire `min(...)` computation in a `try/catch` and treat a revert as a zero-unit result (skip the offer), consistent with the stated design intent of skipping offers that would revert.

## Proof of Concept
1. Deploy `MidnightBundles` against a live or forked Midnight instance with a market where `settlementFee > 0`.
2. As attacker, post a buy offer: `tick=0`, `maxUnits=0`, `maxAssets=1`, `buy=true`.
3. As a legitimate taker, call `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` with a `takes` array containing the attacker's offer alongside valid offers.
4. Observe the entire transaction reverts with an arithmetic underflow panic (Solidity 0.8 checked arithmetic), even though the valid offers would have succeeded individually.
5. Confirm with a Foundry unit test: call `TickLib.tickToPrice(0)` and assert it returns `0`; then call `TakeAmountsLib.buyerAssetsToUnits` with `offerPrice=0` and any `settlementFee>0` and assert it reverts.
