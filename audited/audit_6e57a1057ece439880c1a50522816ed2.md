### Title
Malicious `onBuy` callback increases seller's debt via nested `take`, causing outer `take` to revert with `SellerIsLiquidatable` - (`File: src/Midnight.sol`)

### Summary
In `take()`, the liquidation lock is set for the seller before callbacks are invoked at line 444, but the outer take's health check at line 476 executes after the lock is released at line 475. A malicious buyer's `onBuy` callback can call a nested `take()` on a sell offer where the same seller is the maker, increasing the seller's debt. The nested take passes its own health check because the liquidation lock is still set (`wasLocked = true` in the nested call), but the outer take's health check then fails because the seller's debt is now higher, causing the outer take to revert with `SellerIsLiquidatable`. The `setConsumed` step in the question's framing is not required and is incorrect (`setConsumed` can only increase consumed, never decrease it per line 725's `AlreadyConsumed` guard); the nested-take mechanism alone is sufficient.
