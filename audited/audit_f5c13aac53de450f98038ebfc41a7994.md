### Title
Unconstrained `receiver` in `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` allows authorized caller to redirect victim's credit-redemption proceeds - (File: src/periphery/MidnightBundles.sol)

### Summary

`supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` checks that `msg.sender` is authorized by `taker` (victim), but places no restriction on the `receiver` parameter. An attacker who holds `isAuthorized[victim][attacker] = true` and `isAuthorized[victim][bundler] = true` can call the function with `taker=victim, receiver=attacker`, causing the victim's credit to be redeemed via `take` while all resulting loan tokens are transferred to the attacker.

### Impact Explanation

An attacker who has been granted `isAuthorized` by the victim (and the victim has also authorized the bundler) can fully drain the victim's credit position. The victim's credit units are consumed and the corresponding loan tokens are transferred to the attacker. The victim ends with zero credit and zero loan tokens received. This directly violates the invariant that credit redemption proceeds must go to the position owner.

### Likelihood Explanation

**Preconditions:**
1. Victim has authorized the bundler on Midnight — this is a required step for any user who wants to use `MidnightBundles` legitimately.
2. Victim has authorized the attacker on Midnight — this could occur via a phishing/social-engineering attack, a malicious contract the victim approved, or a compromised key.
3. A valid, non-expired buy offer exists with sufficient `maxUnits` — normal market condition.

Once both authorizations exist, the attack is a single transaction, repeatable until the victim's credit is exhausted, and requires no capital from the attacker.

### Recommendation

Restrict `receiver` to be either `taker` itself or an address explicitly authorized by `taker`. The simplest fix is to replace the caller-supplied `receiver` with `taker` directly, or add:

```solidity
require(receiver == taker || IMidnight(MIDNIGHT).isAuthorized(taker, receiver), Unauthorized());
```

at the top of `supplyCollateralAndSellWithUnitsTarget` (and the analogous `supplyCollateralAndSellWithAssetsTarget`). [6](#0-5)
