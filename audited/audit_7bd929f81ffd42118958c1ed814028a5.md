### Title
No check for sequencer uptime can lead to post-maturity liquidations executing at inflated LIF, causing excess collateral seizure from borrowers - (File: src/Midnight.sol)

---

### Summary

The post-maturity liquidation incentive factor (LIF) ramps linearly from `WAD` (1x) to `maxLif` over `TIME_TO_MAX_LIF = 15 minutes` using `block.timestamp`. On L2 networks, if the sequencer goes offline at or near maturity and comes back after the 15-minute window has elapsed, the LIF immediately reads as `maxLif`. Borrowers had no opportunity to repay during the grace period, yet liquidators can seize maximum collateral as if the full ramp had played out fairly.

---

### Finding Description

In `liquidate()`, the post-maturity LIF is computed as:

```solidity
uint256 lif = postMaturityMode
    ? UtilsLib.min(_maxLif, WAD + (_maxLif - WAD) * (block.timestamp - market.maturity) / TIME_TO_MAX_LIF)
    : _maxLif;
``` [1](#0-0) 

`TIME_TO_MAX_LIF` is hardcoded to 15 minutes: [2](#0-1) 

The design intent is to give borrowers a 15-minute window after maturity to repay at LIF = 1 (no liquidation incentive), with the incentive ramping up to `maxLif` to attract liquidators only gradually. The `live_context.json` confirms this:

> "post_maturity_liquidation_incentive_starts_at": "1", "post_maturity_liquidation_ramp_window": "15 minutes" [3](#0-2) 

The seized collateral is then computed directly from `lif`:

```solidity
seizedAssets = repaidUnits.mulDivDown(lif, WAD).mulDivDown(ORACLE_PRICE_SCALE, liquidatedCollatPrice);
``` [4](#0-3) 

If the L2 sequencer goes offline at maturity and comes back after ≥ 15 minutes, `block.timestamp` reflects real elapsed time. The LIF formula evaluates to `maxLif` immediately on sequencer restart. There is no sequencer uptime check anywhere in `Midnight.sol`. [5](#0-4) 

---

### Impact Explanation

Borrowers lose their entire 15-minute grace period to repay at LIF = 1. Liquidators can immediately seize collateral at `maxLif` the moment the sequencer comes back online.

For the lowest LLTV tier with the high liquidation cursor:

```
LLTV = 0.385, gamma = 0.5
maxLif = 1 / (1 - 0.5 * (1 - 0.385)) = 1 / 0.6925 ≈ 1.444
``` [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6) 

A liquidator repaying 100 units of debt seizes collateral worth ~144.4 units at the oracle price — 44.4% more than the debt repaid — all at the borrower's expense, and with zero opportunity for the borrower to have avoided it.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- The protocol is EVM-compatible and targets L2 deployment (Arbitrum, Optimism, Base, etc. have all experienced sequencer outages).
- The grace window is only **15 minutes** — an extremely tight window. Any sequencer outage spanning maturity that lasts longer than 15 minutes eliminates the entire grace period.
- No sequencer uptime oracle (e.g., Chainlink L2 sequencer feed) is consulted anywhere in the codebase.
- The attacker (liquidator) needs no privileged access — `liquidate()` is permissionless (absent a `liquidatorGate`). [8](#0-7) 

---

### Recommendation

Integrate a Chainlink L2 sequencer uptime feed. In `liquidate()` when `postMaturityMode == true`, check that the sequencer has been live continuously since maturity. If the sequencer was down during the ramp window, either:

1. Revert liquidations until the sequencer has been live for `TIME_TO_MAX_LIF` continuously, or
2. Anchor the LIF ramp start to the sequencer's last restart timestamp rather than `market.maturity`.

Reference: [Chainlink L2 Sequencer Uptime Feeds](https://docs.chain.link/data-feeds/l2-sequencer-feeds)

---

### Proof of Concept

1. A market with `maturity = T` is deployed on an L2 (e.g., Arbitrum). A borrower has a position that becomes liquidatable post-maturity.
2. The L2 sequencer goes offline at time `T` (maturity).
3. The sequencer comes back online at `T + 20 minutes`. `block.timestamp` = `T + 20 min`.
4. A liquidator immediately calls `liquidate(... postMaturityMode = true ...)`.
5. LIF is computed:
   ```
   lif = min(maxLif, WAD + (maxLif - WAD) * (20 min / 15 min))
       = maxLif  (capped, since 20 > 15)
   ```
6. The liquidator seizes collateral at `maxLif` — the maximum possible incentive — even though the borrower had zero on-chain opportunity to repay during the 15-minute grace period.
7. The borrower suffers excess collateral loss compared to what the protocol's ramp design intended. [9](#0-8)
