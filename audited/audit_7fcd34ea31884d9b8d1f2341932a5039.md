Audit Report

## Title
`buyWithAssetsTargetAndWithdrawCollateral` reverts entirely when any sell offer has `tick = MAX_TICK` and non-zero settlement fee - (File: `src/periphery/MidnightBundles.sol`)

## Summary

`buyWithAssetsTargetAndWithdrawCollateral` computes `unitsToTake` via `TakeAmountsLib.buyerAssetsToUnits` at line 209, which executes **before** the `try` keyword at line 215. For any sell offer at `tick = MAX_TICK` (5820) in a market with a non-zero settlement fee, `buyerPrice = WAD + settlementFee > WAD`, triggering the `require(buyerPrice <= WAD)` guard and reverting the entire bundle transaction. A malicious maker can exploit this to permanently grief any taker bundle that includes their offer.

## Finding Description

**Exact code path:**

1. In `buyWithAssetsTargetAndWithdrawCollateral` (lines 205–222), `unitsToTake` is assigned via `min(TakeAmountsLib.buyerAssetsToUnits(...), takes[i].units, ConsumableUnitsLib.consumableUnits(...))` at lines 208–214. All three arguments are evaluated unconditionally before the `try` at line 215. [1](#0-0) 

2. Inside `buyerAssetsToUnits` (`TakeAmountsLib.sol` lines 26–28), for a sell offer (`offer.buy = false`): `sellerPrice = offerPrice` and `buyerPrice = sellerPrice + settlementFee`. The guard `require(buyerPrice <= WAD, TickLib.PriceGreaterThanOne())` is then evaluated. [2](#0-1) 

3. `tickToPrice(MAX_TICK)` returns exactly `WAD` (1e18): with `tick = 5820`, the argument to `wExp` is `LN_ONE_PLUS_DELTA * (2910 - 5820)`, a large negative value, so `wExp(...)` collapses to near zero, and the formula `1e36 / (1e18 + ~0)` rounds to `1e18 = WAD` after the `PRICE_ROUNDING_STEP` quantization. [3](#0-2) 

4. `MAX_TICK = 5820` is a valid, reachable constant. [4](#0-3) 

5. With any `settlementFee > 0`, `buyerPrice = WAD + settlementFee > WAD`, so the `require` reverts. This revert propagates out of the `min(...)` expression, past the `try` keyword, and unwinds the entire transaction.

6. `ConsumableUnitsLib.consumableUnits` does not rescue this: for a sell offer with `maxUnits > 0` it returns `offer.maxUnits.zeroFloorSub(consumed)` directly; for `maxUnits == 0` it calls `sellerAssetsToUnits` (which has no `buyerPrice <= WAD` guard). Neither path preempts the revert from `buyerAssetsToUnits`, which is evaluated as a separate argument to `min(...)` regardless. [5](#0-4) 

**Why existing checks do not stop it:**

- The `try/catch` at lines 215–221 only wraps `IMidnight.take(...)`. The pre-computation of `unitsToTake` is unconditionally outside it. [6](#0-5) 
- The NatSpec at line 175 acknowledges "Reverts if TakeAmountsLib or ConsumableUnitsLib reverts," but line 176 promises "If taking an offer reverts, the bundler will completely skip this offer." The revert from `buyerAssetsToUnits` is not a revert from `take()`, yet it kills the entire bundle, violating the skip guarantee. [7](#0-6) 

## Impact Explanation

The entire `buyWithAssetsTargetAndWithdrawCollateral` transaction reverts. All other valid offers in the `takes[]` array are blocked. The taker's `targetBuyerAssets` transfer (line 197) is also reverted, so funds are not permanently frozen, but the taker's intended purchase is completely blocked for that bundle invocation. A malicious maker can deliberately post a sell offer at `tick = MAX_TICK` in any market with non-zero settlement fee to permanently grief any taker bundle that includes it. [8](#0-7) 

## Likelihood Explanation

The precondition requires only: (1) a sell offer at `tick = MAX_TICK` exists in the market, and (2) the market has a non-zero settlement fee. Both are trivially satisfiable — `MAX_TICK = 5820` is a valid tick accessible at any tick spacing that divides 5820, and non-zero settlement fees are standard. The condition is permanent and structural: it does not depend on market state, timing, or victim mistakes. Any taker who aggregates offers from an order book and includes such an offer triggers the revert.

## Recommendation

Move the `TakeAmountsLib.buyerAssetsToUnits(...)` call inside the `try/catch` block, or wrap it in its own `try/catch` so that a revert from price computation causes the offer to be skipped rather than the entire bundle to revert. One approach is to restructure the loop body so that `unitsToTake` is computed inside the `try` expression (e.g., by introducing an internal helper function that combines the computation and the `take` call), or to catch the revert from `buyerAssetsToUnits` explicitly and `continue` to the next offer.

## Proof of Concept

Minimal fork/unit test plan:

1. Deploy a market with a non-zero settlement fee (e.g., `settlementFee > 0`).
2. Have a malicious maker post a sell offer at `tick = MAX_TICK` (5820) with any valid `maxUnits` or `maxAssets`.
3. Have a legitimate taker call `buyWithAssetsTargetAndWithdrawCollateral` with a `takes[]` array that includes the malicious offer alongside other valid offers.
4. Observe that the entire transaction reverts with `PriceGreaterThanOne()` rather than skipping the malicious offer and filling the others.
5. Confirm that removing the malicious offer from `takes[]` allows the transaction to succeed, proving the grief vector.
