### Title
`rcfThreshold` Can Be Set to Zero at Market Creation, Permanently Disabling RCF Deactivation for Small Positions — (File: src/Midnight.sol)

---

### Summary

The `touchMarket` function validates `lltv` and `maxLif` for each collateral but applies **no validation to `market.rcfThreshold`**. A market created with `rcfThreshold = 0` permanently disables the Recovery Close Factor (RCF) deactivation mechanism for small positions. This means small unhealthy positions can never be fully liquidated in a single normal-mode transaction, making them unprofitable to liquidate and causing bad debt to accumulate and be socialized among lenders.

---

### Finding Description

**Root cause — missing validation in `touchMarket`:**

`touchMarket` enforces strict checks on `lltv` (whitelist) and `maxLif` (formula), but `rcfThreshold` is accepted without any lower-bound check: [1](#0-0) 

`rcfThreshold` is part of the `Market` struct and is baked into the market ID at creation time, making it **immutable for the lifetime of the market**: [2](#0-1) 

**How `rcfThreshold` is consumed in `liquidate`:**

In normal-mode liquidation, the RCF check is: [3](#0-2) 

The second disjunct — `collateralValue/lif - maxRepaid < market.rcfThreshold` — is the **deactivation condition**: it allows a liquidator to exceed `maxRepaid` when the remaining collateral is too small to cover gas costs. With `rcfThreshold = 0`, this condition evaluates to `uint256_value < 0`, which is **always false** in Solidity. The RCF is therefore **never deactivated**, and every normal-mode liquidation is hard-capped at `maxRepaid` units regardless of position size.

**Code comment confirming the design intent:** [4](#0-3) 

The protocol explicitly documents that `rcfThreshold` exists to make small positions fully liquidatable in one transaction. Setting it to 0 silently voids this guarantee.

---

### Impact Explanation

With `rcfThreshold = 0`:

1. A small unhealthy position (e.g., a few hundred wei of debt) can only be partially liquidated per transaction, capped at `maxRepaid`.
2. `maxRepaid = (debt − maxDebt) × WAD² / (WAD² − lif × lltv)` — for a tiny position this is a tiny number.
3. The gas cost of repeated partial liquidations exceeds the liquidation incentive.
4. No rational liquidator will touch the position.
5. The position's bad debt is eventually socialized among all lenders in the market via the `lossFactor` mechanism. [5](#0-4) 

Lenders suffer a loss they cannot avoid, caused by a parameter they had no ability to validate before entering the market.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

`touchMarket` is **permissionless** — any address can create a market. A market creator (whether malicious or simply careless) can set `rcfThreshold = 0`. Because the market ID is derived from all parameters including `rcfThreshold`, users who interact with the market have no on-chain enforcement preventing them from entering a market with this misconfiguration. The misconfiguration is silent: the market operates normally until small positions become unhealthy, at which point the liquidation gap is revealed. No privileged access is required.

---

### Recommendation

Add a non-zero check for `rcfThreshold` inside the market-creation branch of `touchMarket`:

```solidity
require(market.rcfThreshold > 0, InvalidRcfThreshold());
```

This mirrors the existing pattern used for `lltv` and `maxLif` validation and ensures the RCF deactivation mechanism is always operative. [6](#0-5) 

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy `Midnight` and call `touchMarket` with a valid `Market` struct where `rcfThreshold = 0`. The call succeeds — no revert.
2. A lender supplies credit; a borrower takes a small loan (e.g., 100 units of debt).
3. The oracle price drops so that `debt > maxDebt` (position is unhealthy).
4. A liquidator calls `liquidate` with `repaidUnits = 100` (full debt).
5. `maxRepaid` computes to, say, 5 units (tiny position).
6. The second RCF condition: `collateralValue/lif - 5 < 0` → always false (uint256).
7. `require(100 <= 5 || false)` → **reverts with `RecoveryCloseFactorConditionsViolated`**.
8. The liquidator can only repay 5 units per call. With 100 units of debt, 20 transactions are needed. Gas cost > incentive → position is abandoned.
9. Bad debt accumulates and is socialized among lenders via `lossFactor`. [7](#0-6)
