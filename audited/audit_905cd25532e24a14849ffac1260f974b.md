### Title
Incorrect `_clampUserSums` Detection Allows Pre-Risk Deposits to Bypass Time-Floor, Inflating Bonus Share — (File: src/ConfidencePool.sol)

### Summary
`_clampUserSums` uses the condition `userSumStakeTime[u] < eligibleStake[u] * riskWindowStart` to detect whether a user's stale pre-risk sums need to be promoted to `riskWindowStart`. When a user also holds post-risk deposits, those contributions push `userSumStakeTime` above the threshold, causing the guard to return false even though the pre-risk deposit still needs clamping. The user's k=2 score is then computed with the original (earlier) entry time for the pre-risk tranche, inflating `userScore` above `globalScore` and allowing the user to claim more than 100 % of the bonus pool.

### Finding Description
`_markRiskWindowStart` eagerly resets the **global** accumulators for all current stakers:

```solidity
sumStakeTime  = totalEligibleStake * t;   // every staker treated as entering at S
sumStakeTimeSq = totalEligibleStake * t * t;
``` [1](#0-0) 

Per-user sums are left stale and are corrected lazily by `_clampUserSums`:

```solidity
if (userSumStakeTime[u] < stake_ * start) {
    userSumStakeTime[u] = stake_ * start;
    userSumStakeTimeSq[u] = stake_ * start * start;
}
``` [2](#0-1) 

The guard assumes `userSumStakeTime < totalStake × S` holds if and only if clamping has not yet occurred. This is only true when **all** of the user's deposits pre-date the risk window. When the user also has a post-risk deposit of amount B at time t₂ > S, the `stake()` path records it at wall-clock time:

```solidity
uint256 newEntry = block.timestamp;
uint256 start = riskWindowStart;
if (start != 0 && newEntry < start) newEntry = start;
// ...
userSumStakeTime[msg.sender] += received * newEntry;
``` [3](#0-2) 

After this second deposit the user's sum is `A·t₁ + B·t₂`. The clamping condition becomes:

```
A·t₁ + B·t₂  <  (A+B)·S
```

Because t₂ > S, a sufficiently large B makes the left side exceed the right side, so the condition is **false** and no clamping occurs. The pre-risk tranche A is never promoted to S.

At claim time `_bonusShare` computes:

```solidity
uint256 userPlus  = T * T * userEligible + userSumStakeTimeSq[u];
uint256 userMinus = 2 * T * userSumStakeTime[u];
uint256 userScore = userPlus > userMinus ? userPlus - userMinus : 0;
``` [4](#0-3) 

Because `userSumStakeTime` is smaller than the correctly-clamped value (t₁ < S), `userScore` is **larger** than it should be. Meanwhile `globalScore` was computed from the correctly-reset global sums, so `userScore > globalScore` is possible, and `Math.mulDiv(userScore, snapshotTotalBonus, globalScore)` returns a value exceeding `snapshotTotalBonus`. [5](#0-4) 

### Impact Explanation
A staker who holds both a pre-risk and a sufficiently large post-risk deposit can claim a bonus share that exceeds the entire bonus pool. In a multi-staker pool this drains the bonus pool early, leaving later claimants unable to receive their full payout (their `safeTransfer` reverts because the contract balance is exhausted). In a single-staker pool the attacker's own claim reverts, locking their principal until `sweepUnclaimedBonus` is called, but the design guarantee that "pre-risk capital earns no time weight" is violated regardless.

### Likelihood Explanation
The trigger condition — holding deposits on both sides of the risk window — is a completely ordinary usage pattern. The design explicitly supports top-ups after the risk window opens. The attacker only needs `B·(t₂ − S) ≥ A·(S − t₁)`, which is trivially satisfied by sizing the post-risk deposit appropriately. No privileged role is required; any unprivileged staker can reach this path.

### Recommendation
Replace the sum-comparison heuristic with a per-user boolean flag that is set to `true` the first time `_clampUserSums` successfully clamps the user, and checked on every subsequent call. Alternatively, track the user's pre-risk and post-risk sums in separate accumulators so the clamping can be applied only to the pre-risk portion without disturbing the post-risk portion.

### Proof of Concept
Concrete numbers (timestamps in seconds, amounts in token units):

| Event | t | Alice's eligibleStake | userSumStakeTime | userSumStakeTimeSq |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Alice stakes A=100 | t₁=1 000 | 100 | 100 000 | 100 000 000 |
| Risk window opens S=2 000 | — | — | global reset only | — |
| Alice stakes B=1 000 | t₂=3 000 | 1 100 | 3 100 000 | 9 100 000 000 |

Clamping check: `3 100 000 < 1 100 × 2 000 = 2 200 000`? **No** → no clamping.

Global sums after the second deposit:
- `sumStakeTime = 100×2000 + 1000×3000 = 3 200 000`
- `sumStakeTimeSq = 100×4 000 000 + 1000×9 000 000 = 9 400 000 000`

At resolution T = 4 000, `snapshotTotalBonus = 1 000`:

```
userScore   = 4000²×1100 − 2×4000×3 100 000 + 9 100 000 000
            = 17 600 000 000 − 24 800 000 000 + 9 100 000 000
            = 1 900 000 000

globalScore = 4000²×1100 − 2×4000×3 200 000 + 9 400 000 000
            = 17 600 000 000 − 25 600 000 000 + 9 400 000 000
            = 1 400 000 000

bonusShare  = mulDiv(1 900 000 000, 1 000, 1 400 000 000) = 1 357
```

Alice attempts to receive `1 100 (stake) + 1 357 (bonus) = 2 457` tokens, but the contract holds only `1 100 + 1 000 = 2 100`. The transfer reverts, locking Alice's principal. In a multi-staker pool where Alice claims first, she receives 1 357 bonus tokens (357 more than her fair share), and the remaining stakers find the bonus pool short by 357 tokens, causing their claims to revert.
